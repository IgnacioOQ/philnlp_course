# 🎲 Unit 2 — Hidden Markov Models

**Status:** ⬜ Planned

## Learning goal

Introduce *latent state*: the idea that observable tokens are generated by an underlying, unobserved process. This is the first time the course distinguishes "what we see" from "what we infer."

## Planned contents

- Markov chains as a warm-up — transitions over observable states.
- Hidden Markov models: emissions, transitions, initial distribution.
- Three classic problems and their algorithms:
  - **Likelihood** — forward algorithm.
  - **Decoding** — Viterbi.
  - **Learning** — Baum–Welch (EM).
- A worked example: part-of-speech tagging on toy sentences.
- Philosophical interlude: latent variables and the inference of unobservables.

## Prerequisite

- [`01_ngrams/`](../01_ngrams/) — for the language-modeling baseline this generalizes.
